Questions

How fresh is the data?
Most leads are 1-3 days old. We scan every week and only include roles posted in the last 7 days. Stale listings get dropped automatically.
What does "scored" mean?
Every lead gets a score based on three things: seniority (C-level scores higher than VP), hiring signals (reports to CEO, building a team, growth hire), and salary budget ($300K+ scores higher than $150K). Higher scores mean higher-value retained search opportunities. The scoring breakdown is fully transparent.
Where does the data come from?
We scan major job boards for VP+ executive roles with posted compensation. We extract signals from the full job description that you'd normally have to read manually: reporting structure, team-building mandates, growth context, urgency indicators.
What's in the Excel workbook?
Every lead from the week with 15 columns: title, company, location, salary range, score, signals, source URL, and more. Color-coded by score tier, with clickable source links and auto-filters. Sort and filter however you need.
Is this just scraped job boards?
The raw postings come from job boards. But a job posting and a scored lead are different things. We extract the signals, confirm salary budgets, score by retained-search relevance, and add market context. That's the work you'd otherwise do manually, or miss entirely.
